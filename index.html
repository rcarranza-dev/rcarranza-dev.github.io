My path into security started in the data center at Milestone Technologies — learning how physical infrastructure works at scale before moving into IT support at Core Machinery. There I had direct exposure to two significant security incidents: a ransomware attack where I led internal response communications, and a CEO impersonation BEC fraud that highlighted the human element in security failures. Those experiences pointed me toward network security, and today I work at American Express managing Palo Alto firewall infrastructure across on-premises and multi-cloud environments.

Outside of work I'm usually at the gym, out on the golf course, or deep in a sci-fi novel. I've always been drawn to big picture thinking — whether that's the ethics of AI in science fiction, the scale of the universe through astronomy, or the intersection of emerging technology and human behavior. That same curiosity is what keeps pulling me toward the evolving edges of security.
